T001 is clean. Naming the deliveries table webhook_endpoint_deliveries to avoid collision with the existing webhook_delivery_log (which belongs to the enrichment system) is the right call. Don't touch the other system's tables.
T002 — one thing to watch. The deliverWithRetry delays are [0, 60_000, 300_000] — immediate, 1 minute, 5 minutes. Those retries happen in-process with setTimeout. If the server restarts during a retry window, the pending retries are lost. That's fine for v1 — the circuit breaker and delivery log mean you'll know about failures even if retries don't complete. A proper job queue is a future enhancement, not a blocker.
T005 is the most important task. The significant-change filter (|scoreChange| >= 5 OR gradeChanged) is exactly what I recommended. On Frontera's 5,089 contacts, a typical scoring run where CRM data shifted slightly might produce 50-200 webhook events instead of 5,000. That's manageable for any downstream endpoint.
The field mapping from ProspectScoreResult to ScoredEntity is where bugs will hide. The source, sourceId, and sourceObject fields need to come from the underlying deal/contact records, not from the scoring output. Replit correctly notes to look these up from dealFeatures/contactFeatures. Worth verifying that those arrays are still in scope at the point where the emitter gets called — if they've been garbage collected after the persist step, you'll need to either pass them through or query them.
T006 — the secret-reveal modal is a critical UX detail. The secret is returned exactly once on creation. If the user closes that modal without copying the secret, they have to delete and recreate the endpoint. That's intentional (same pattern as Stripe, GitHub, etc.) but the warning needs to be prominent. "This secret will not be shown again" in bold, with a copy button that confirms the copy succeeded.
T007 verification is thorough. The HMAC verification step is particularly good — confirms the signing works end-to-end, not just that the delivery succeeded. One addition: after the scoring run, check the console.log output for the [Webhooks] Emitted X prospect.scored events to Y endpoints message. That confirms the wiring in lead-scoring.ts is actually executing, not silently failing in the .catch().
