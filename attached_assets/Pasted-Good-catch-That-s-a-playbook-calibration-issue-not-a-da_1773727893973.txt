Good catch. That's a playbook calibration issue, not a data bug.

**What's happening:** `pipeline-coverage` is reporting 0.82x open pipeline against a 3.0x coverage target. That's technically correct — $288K open pipeline divided by whatever quota is configured. But **3.0x coverage is a planning metric**, not a late-quarter execution metric. With 14 days left in Q1 and $1.3M already closed, nobody cares about coverage. The question is: did we hit quota, and what can still close in 14 days?

The Orchestrator doesn't know what week of the quarter it is when deciding which metrics to lead with. It's treating a coverage gap warning the same way in week 12 as it would in week 2.

**The fix is in the playbook, not the skill.** In `server/orchestrator/playbooks.ts`, update the `monday_briefing` playbook to add quarter-position awareness:

```
QUARTER POSITION RULES:
- Weeks 1–8 (>6 weeks remaining): Lead with pipeline coverage
  and gap to 3x target. Coverage IS the story.
- Weeks 9–11 (3–6 weeks remaining): Lead with forecast landing
  zone. Coverage is context, not headline.
- Weeks 12–13 (<3 weeks remaining): Lead with closed-won vs
  quota only. Open pipeline coverage is IRRELEVANT — omit it
  entirely. The only number that matters is what's closed and
  what can realistically close before quarter end.

Current quarter position: {{days_remaining_in_quarter}} days left.
Apply the appropriate rule above.
```

Then pass `days_remaining_in_quarter` into the user message — which is already happening via `workspace_context`. The Orchestrator just needs the instruction to use it for metric prioritization.

With 14 days left, the correct headline would be something like:

> "Frontera Health has closed $1.3M this quarter — confirm whether that hits target and identify what of the $288K open pipeline can close before March 31."

That's the right question for week 12. Coverage against a 3x target is next quarter's problem.
