Yes — significantly. And what you're seeing in the screenshots makes the overlap concrete. Three separate systems all asking "tell me how your business works":

**What each one does:**

The **Setup Workspace Interview** (Image 3) is a Tier 0 inferred configuration flow. It reads your CRM, makes inferences, and asks you to confirm or correct them. Five steps: Motions (which pipelines are real selling motions), Calendar (fiscal year, quarter definitions), Stages (stage names and their funnel position), Team (rep hierarchy), Delivery (Slack, notifications). It stores answers in `workspace_config` as structured configuration.

The **Calibration Interview** is a 6-step filter definition flow. It asks what counts as active pipeline, at-risk, committed, and how you define win rate and coverage. Stores confirmed filter definitions in `business_dimensions` and metric thresholds in `metric_definitions`.

The **Data Dictionary** is a manually curated glossary of terms, metrics, filters, and stage definitions. Currently separate from both — nothing writes to it automatically.

**Where they overlap:**

```

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

real selling motions?   →    pipeline?            →    (currently manual)

                             at-risk? committed?  →    (currently manual)
```

The Stages step in Setup Interview and the Stage Mapping step in Calibration are asking the same question through different interfaces. A user who completes both is doing redundant work. And neither one writes to the Data Dictionary — so a third surface exists that captures none of what the first two confirmed.

**The right mental model:**

These aren't three separate features — they're three phases of the same onboarding job:

```
Phase 1: DISCOVER (Setup Interview)
  Pandora reads your CRM and infers your structure.
  You confirm or correct the inferences.
  Output: workspace_config with motions, stages, team

Phase 2: DEFINE (Calibration Interview)  
  Pandora asks how you measure the business.
  You confirm filter and metric definitions.
  Output: business_dimensions, metric_definitions

Phase 3: DOCUMENT (Data Dictionary)
  Everything confirmed in Phase 1 + 2 auto-populates here.
  Humans can add additional terms manually.
  Output: single source of truth for all definitions
```

Right now Phase 1 and Phase 2 exist as separate interview flows with separate storage, and Phase 3 is completely disconnected from both. The Setup Guide checklist (with its 10 steps) is a fourth surface trying to orchestrate the first two without knowing about them.
